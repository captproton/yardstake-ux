The Jurisdiction Intake

Zara runs the Shapefile through the GeometryValidator. It surfaces 94 self-intersecting polygons and 12 parcels with mismatched SRIDs. She resolves them, re-projects to the correct CRS, and commits the first clean batch. Eugene is now in the database.

The Rulebook

Zara reads the ADU ordinance line by line and translates it into the strategy pattern. Setbacks: 4ft rear, 4ft side. Max height: 15ft. Lot coverage: 50%. Parking waiver: within half a mile of a major transit stop. She writes the integration tests first. All green.

The Confidence Calibration

The first 200 feasibility queries come in. Zara reviews the distribution. Clear lots with confirmed zoning and complete geometry land in High. Overlay zones and missing utility easements fall to Medium. Historic preservation districts and flood-zone conflicts drop to Low — routed to a human expert before any homeowner sees a green light.

The Feedback Loop

Six months in, twelve transactions have closed in Eugene. Zara runs the accuracy audit: 11 of 11 High-confidence predictions resulted in a completed build. One Medium-confidence project hit an unexpected variance requirement. She narrows the Medium-to-Low threshold. The Digital Twin gets measurably smarter with each house that lands.
