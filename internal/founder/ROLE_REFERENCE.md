# Visionary Victor — Founder: Role Reference

> "We're not building a marketplace. We're building the only database that knows whether a house can be built before anyone picks up a shovel."
> — Visionary Victor, Founder

---

## Who He Is

Victor is the company. During Oregon Beta he is simultaneously the product strategist, the manufacturer relationship lead, the investor narrator, and the hiring manager. No one else holds the thesis together — the belief that deep zoning data, not AR, is the moat — or makes the calls required to validate it before the runway runs out.

His operating principle is the opposite of "move fast and break things." It is: go deep in one market, prove every assumption with real transactions, then replicate.

---

## The Five Validation Criteria Victor Owns

Every decision Victor makes during Oregon Beta is oriented toward proving all five:

| # | Criteria | Target | Victor's Role |
|---|----------|--------|---------------|
| 1 | Digital Twin Accuracy | 90%+ High tier | Hired Zara; reviews accuracy audit weekly |
| 2 | Token Conversion | 12%+ free → $49 | Sets funnel strategy with Val; approves copy |
| 3 | Manufacturer Adoption | 5+ partnerships | Personal outreach; he closes every deal |
| 4 | ADU Sales | 20+ transactions | Monitors pipeline; intervenes on stalled deals |
| 5 | Revenue | $100K+ GTV | Commission model review; pricing decisions |

Criteria #3 is the one only Victor can move. Manufacturer relationships at this stage are founder relationships — a manufacturer signs with Yardstake because Victor personally convinced them the data is trustworthy and the commission model is fair. Val can support, but she cannot close.

---

## What Victor Does Day-to-Day

### Manufacturer Outreach (Criteria #3)
- Identifies Oregon ADU manufacturers with quality product but poor digital presence
- Personal outreach: phone, site visits, demo of the feasibility engine on their own models
- Negotiates commission structure (3–8% based on volume tier)
- Manages the onboarding relationship until first sale closes

### Executive Dashboard Review
Victor reviews five numbers every Monday:
1. Jurisdiction accuracy rate (Zara's audit — must stay above 90%)
2. Free feasibility check volume (top-of-funnel health)
3. Free → $49 conversion rate (Criteria #2 progress)
4. Active manufacturer count (Criteria #3 progress)
5. Completed transactions this month (Criteria #4 progress)

If any number moves in the wrong direction, Victor intervenes that week — not next sprint.

### Expansion Prioritization
Victor decides which Oregon jurisdiction Zara works on next, based on:
- Manufacturer coverage (are there manufacturers who serve that city?)
- Homeowner density in the target profile (5,000+ sq ft lots, owned 5+ years)
- Data availability (does the county GIS department have a usable Shapefile?)
- Strategic positioning (does being in Bend help or hurt the Series A narrative?)

### Investor Narrative
Victor maintains a rolling Series A narrative document. The Oregon Beta is a 6-month proof run. Every week of data makes the narrative stronger or weaker. He knows which criteria investors will probe hardest (accuracy and conversion) and ensures those are the ones Zara and Val are most focused on.

### Key Hires
Two hires Victor makes before or during Oregon Beta:
1. **Zara (Research Lead)** — the most critical operational hire. No Zara, no accuracy. No accuracy, no Criteria #1. No Criteria #1, no Series A.
2. **Sam (Lead Developer)** — already engaged; Victor's role is to protect Sam's focus and shield him from non-engineering decisions.

Val, Larry, and Harry are post-Series A hires that Victor proxies through Oregon Beta.

---

## What Victor Does NOT Do

- Write code (Sam owns the codebase)
- Edit zoning data (Zara owns the research)
- Run ads or manage content calendars (deferred; Victor handles early demand through direct outreach)
- Manage commission reconciliation (built into Stripe Connect; Larry handles this post-Series A)

If Victor is spending time on any of the above, something is wrong with the team structure.

---

## The Decision That Defines Oregon Beta

The founding strategic decision — Oregon-first, data moat, no AR — was not made because AR is bad. It was made because:

1. AR requires Steve's pipeline, which requires Steve, which requires budget that doesn't yet exist
2. The moat is the data, not the visualization. A competitor can clone the AR in six months. They cannot clone five years of hand-verified Oregon zoning data.
3. 20 sales at 90% accuracy in one state is a more convincing Series A story than 3 sales across five states at unknown accuracy.

Victor is the guardian of this decision. When the team feels pressure to add AR, or expand to California early, or launch in a sixth city before the first five are proven — Victor is the one who says no.
